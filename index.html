Gameplay

How It Works

01

Dash vs. Water

Two players, two completely different abilities. Player 1 dashes, closing the gap instantly and punishing anyone who stands still. Player 2 launches water projectiles, controlling the space between them and forcing their opponent to keep moving. Neither ability beats the other. They just play differently.

02

Opposite Abilities, Equal Footing

Player 1 closes distance. Player 2 creates it. One ability is useless without the other to challenge it, you need an opponent who plays differently to make either one matter. That's the whole game, and that's the theme: Better Together.

Dev Log

Behind the Game

Ghost Tide was built for the CIS Academy Spring Game Jam 2026 around the theme "Better Together." The game is a 1v1 fighter, but the theme is into the design. A dash is only dangerous if there's someone to dash at. A water projectile only matters if there's someone to dodge it. The two abilities were built against each other on purpose. You need an opponent to make either one meaningful.

Six students across three grade levels built this: two juniors, two sophomores, two freshmen. The storyboard below shows how the game was planned before a single line of code was written.
